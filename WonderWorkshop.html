Wonder Workshop

OVERVIEW

THE TEAM

Six senior undergraduate Tufts students: Two Human Factors Engineers, One Computer Engineer, Two Electrical Engineers, and One Computer Scientist

THE SPONSORS

Wonder Workshop, Tufts University School of Engineering

THE OBJECTIVE

To build a desktop-based user interface that allows two-way communication between the Cue Robot and the Micro:bit for middle school students to learn computer science in a fun and interactive manner.

MY ROLE

To provide user research, to design and conduct usability tests, and to organize and relay findings to the engineering team for improvements in future iteration.

Phase 1

USER RESEARCH

FIELD EXPERT INTERVIEWS

CONTEXT

In order to grasp the context of computer science learning in middle schools, we needed to understand the educators and creators of coding curriculum. We read educator blogs, twitter posts, and teacher forums. We familiarized ourselves with these online communities of educators and creators. We then sought out the experts in these communities for phone and e-mail based interviews. We spoke to middle school educators with diverse backgrounds in both robotics and computer science, as well as curriculum creators and facilitators.

FINDINGS

Need for Inclusivity: Not all students learn at the same pace. Our user interface must accommodate a range of intellectual abilities.

Need for Excitement: Students get bored. The most effective technologies induce excitement and participation. Our user interface must provide several kinds of stimuli (haptic, visual and audio) and provide appropriate feedback.

Need for Collaboration: Robots are expensive. Engineering classes and clubs are often not prioritized. Most schools lack sufficient funding to bring in technology, like robots, into the classroom. Funding frequently comes from external sources, like PTA's, but they often only provide a few per class. Our user interface must permit multiple students to work together simultaneously.

PERSONAE CREATION

JENNA

Jenna is in the 6th grade and has seen her older brother coding a lot. She is shy but excited by the idea of computer science and robotics. She sometimes struggles to understand concepts in a traditional classroom lecturing style, and prefers visual learning and physical activities.

CRAIG

Craig is computer science savant. He has had the luxury of attending an after-school computer science club and is ahead of the curve academically. He gets impatient easily, and is bored by the pace of the material taught in class. Craig seeks accelerated learning that matches his experience and ability.

TOM

Tom is a 7th grade engineering teacher at Chatham Middle School. He loves teaching this age group because they are so impressionable and excited by learning. He wants his students to have the proper tools and technologies they deserve, spending a lot of his energy advocating and requesting for funding that often is unavailable. The devices that he does have to help him teach often need software updates or become obsolete.
