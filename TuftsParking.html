THE OBJECTIVE

To identify and explore the depths of parking at Tufts, which has become a wicked problem, an unsolvable dilemma.

MY ROLE

Survey and interview stakeholders, design the brochure, and produce the final video.

Part 1: Brochure

RESEARCH

SURVEYS

Designed in Google Forms

We sent our simple five question survey to Tufts Facebook groups targeting students who park on campus. Our survey's purpose was two fold: to understand the kind of passes people were buying, how many tickets people were receiving, etc. and to gauge student satisfaction with the current parking situation. We received over 151 responses in two days, using the data collected as leverage for our brochure.

TAKEAWAYS

Interviews can be uncomfortable. Filming doesn't help. As the interviewers, it is imperative that we are approachable. Particularly in this case, we wanted honest opinions and insight. Our questions and demeanour needed to demonstrate neutrality.

People want to be heard. Listening to other point of views brings perspective. Parking at Tufts is a problem for everyone: for those who park and for those who manage it. Each person brings a unique story to give a clearer picture at the problem at hand.

Wicked Problems can be managed Innovation and Collaboration are Key. Through organized design plans and research, even some of the most impossible of problems can be minimized. We should encourage exploration of nuanced solutions that balance the needs of those affected.
